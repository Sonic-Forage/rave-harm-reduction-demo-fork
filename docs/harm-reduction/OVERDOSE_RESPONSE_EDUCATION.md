# Overdose Response Education Notes

This document is a pointer to qualified training, not a replacement for training.

SAMHSA’s Overdose Prevention and Response Toolkit provides guidance on preventing and responding to overdoses, including opioid overdose reversal medications such as naloxone and nalmefene.

## Public-safe repo stance

- Encourage people to get trained by qualified local organizations.
- Encourage calling emergency services in suspected overdose/emergency situations.
- Keep naloxone information source-backed and local-law aware.
- Never claim repo docs make someone medically trained.

## Venue / community checklist

- Emergency escalation route is known.
- Local qualified training/resource links are listed.
- Staff know where emergency services meet them.
- People are told early help is welcome and nonjudgmental.

## U.S. help resource

SAMHSA National Helpline: **1-800-662-HELP (4357)**. FindTreatment.gov can help locate treatment services.
