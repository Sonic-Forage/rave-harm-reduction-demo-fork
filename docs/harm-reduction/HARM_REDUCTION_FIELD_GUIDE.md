# Harm Reduction Field Guide for Rave / Nightlife Communities

Harm reduction is practical, nonjudgmental care: reducing preventable harm while respecting the dignity, autonomy, and real conditions of people in a community.

## Core principles adapted for nightlife

Based on National Harm Reduction Coalition principles:

- Meet people where they are; do not shame or coerce.
- Center quality of life, safety, and well-being.
- Let people with lived experience shape policy and practice.
- Recognize poverty, racism, stigma, trauma, access, and policing shape risk.
- Do not minimize real dangers; communicate them clearly.

## Rave/nightlife translation

A safer dance-space culture includes:

- hydration access and chill-out spaces
- accessible exits and rest areas
- consent culture and bystander support
- non-policing care teams where possible
- evidence-based overdose education
- naloxone access where legal/appropriate
- drug-checking education where legal/appropriate
- clear “get help early” messaging
- source-backed local resource lists

## What this repo should not do

- It does not diagnose, treat, or train emergency medical response.
- It does not encourage or facilitate illegal activity.
- It does not publish private event logistics or sensitive scene details.
- It does not replace local public-health/legal guidance.

## Public-safe language pattern

Use:

> “This is educational harm-reduction information. Follow local laws, consult qualified trainers/public-health organizations, and call emergency services in an emergency.”

Avoid:

> “This will keep everyone safe.”

Better:

> “This may reduce preventable risk when implemented with trained local partners.”
