# City Resource Directory Template

Use this template when turning the finished demo fork into a local, public-safe resource directory. Keep it source-backed, nonjudgmental, and documentation-only until an awake human organizer approves any external action.

> Safety note: this template is educational. It is **not medical advice**, legal advice, emergency response training, or a claim that any listed group provides a verified service for a specific event. If someone may be in danger, contact local emergency services immediately.

## Closed-by-default status

- `status`: `closed_until_human_yes`
- Public posting/outreach: closed until human approval
- Fundraising, sponsorships, payment links, or spending: closed until human approval
- Field operations, staffing, volunteer dispatch, or service promises: closed until human approval
- Private event locations, incident reports, rosters, or media uploads: closed until human approval
- Medical/legal advice, diagnosis, certification, or training claims: blocked

## Source rules

Only add resources that can be backed by public, official, or clearly attributable pages. Prefer:

- city/county/state public health departments;
- public library or community-services pages;
- official crisis, mental-health, or substance-use referral pages;
- harm-reduction organizations with public program descriptions;
- venue-neutral safer-nightlife education sources such as DanceSafe, National Harm Reduction Coalition, CDC, SAMHSA, or local public health pages.

Do not add private group chats, private addresses, unpublished event logistics, personal phone numbers, volunteer rosters, private venue contacts, or unreviewed claims that a service will be present at an event.

## Directory entry pattern

Copy this block for each public resource:

```md
### [RESOURCE NAME]

- Category: `[public health | harm reduction | mental health | emergency | transportation | accessibility | other]`
- Public URL: `[OFFICIAL PUBLIC URL]`
- Source owner/publisher: `[AGENCY OR ORGANIZATION]`
- What the source says, in plain language: `[ONE OR TWO SENTENCES, NO MEDICAL/LEGAL ADVICE]`
- Last checked: `[YYYY-MM-DD]`
- Local relevance: `[CITY/REGION, BROAD ONLY — NO PRIVATE EVENT LOCATION]`
- Caveat: Verify availability, hours, eligibility, and emergency guidance directly with the public source.
```

## Review checklist before publishing a directory

- [ ] Every entry has a public URL and source owner.
- [ ] No private event location, private venue contact, roster, group chat, or incident detail is included.
- [ ] Wording says what the source states; it does not diagnose, prescribe, certify, promise staffing, or guarantee safety.
- [ ] Emergency language tells readers to contact local emergency services when someone may be in danger.
- [ ] A human reviewer has checked the exact public text and recorded approval.

## Example safe entry

### SAMHSA National Helpline

- Category: mental health / substance-use referral
- Public URL: `https://www.samhsa.gov/find-help/national-helpline`
- Source owner/publisher: SAMHSA
- What the source says, in plain language: SAMHSA describes the National Helpline as a confidential treatment referral and information service for people and families facing mental or substance-use disorders.
- Last checked: `[YYYY-MM-DD]`
- Local relevance: U.S. national referral resource; verify local availability and emergency options separately.
- Caveat: This entry is informational and not medical advice. In an emergency, contact local emergency services.

## Human approval question

“Do you approve publishing this exact resource directory text, with these exact URLs, after confirming no private logistics, medical/legal/training claims, outreach, fundraising, or field operations are implied?”

If the answer is missing or ambiguous, keep the directory as a local draft with `closed_until_human_yes`.
